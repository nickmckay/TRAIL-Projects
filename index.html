TRAIL Projects

Teaching, Research, and AI Learning — Northern Arizona University

TRAIL (Transformation through Artificial Intelligence in Learning) is a Northern Arizona University grant program, sponsored by the Office of the Provost, that funds faculty to explore generative AI in their teaching and research. Each awardee receives $5,000 and elevated access to Microsoft Copilot to pilot innovative AI applications in their courses or scholarly work. The program runs two tracks: an Instructional Track focused on student learning outcomes and a Research & Scholarship Track focused on advancing faculty research using AI methods.

The projects below represent two cohort years. The 2024-25 cohort was exclusively instructional, with 10 faculty projects spanning disciplines from Spanish language learning to software engineering. The 2025-26 cohort expanded the program to include a dedicated Research & Scholarship Track alongside 14 new instructional projects across six NAU colleges.
